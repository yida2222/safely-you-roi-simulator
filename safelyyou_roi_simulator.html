How it's calculated

Whose ROI: the operator's. Every line is a dollar the community keeps or earns — not the resident's medical bill.

1 · Resident retention (the big lever). Longer stay → fewer move-outs/yr → fewer empty-unit days & turn costs. move-outs avoided × (vacancy days × daily rate + turn cost). Modeled as avoided vacancy, not gross bed-months, so it holds up with a CFO. Higher refill-vacancy = bigger benefit (slow-lease-up markets).

2 · Fall response labor: falls avoided × staff cost per fall.

3 · Liability / insurance: annual fall liability × fall reduction %.

4 · ER/EMS coordination labor: ER events avoided × staff cost per event.

5–6 · Optional upside (off by default): night rounding hours and reputation-driven occupancy.

No double-counting: the revenue effect of fewer hospitalizations is captured once, inside LOS/retention. Fall & ER lines carry only operational (labor + liability) dollars.
